About Me

I am an artist living in Greenville, South Carolina and working in the food and beverage industry, seeking a position as a software developer.

After receiving my BFA in Painting & Drawing from the University of Georgia in 2011, I taught English for two years at Jeonju Elementary School in the college town of Jeonju, South Korea.

I returned to the United States and moved to Atlanta where I started working in restaurants and volunteering during my free time. Primarily, I taught art to individuals overcoming homelessness through Central Outreach and Advocacy Center's Art Ministry program after reading about it in the news. For four years, I was teaching art classes downtown at Central Presbyterian Church across the street from the capitol building—in the gymnasium where the men slept, or downstairs in the lobby where guests waited for social services.

My interest in programming began as a teenager in an elective computer science class, making my first websites for fun. In my eyes, software development is a creative process, and good craftsmanship is essential. Like painting a picture, developing code is a process of experimenting, refining, and improving one's technical skills to express one's ideas. It involves creativity in problem solving, meticulous attention to detail, and the ability to adapt to endlessly changing tools and technology.

Although I like to make things and enjoy a rewarding technical challenge—I am passionate about good service. For me, service to others is a way of life, and it is how I contribute to the well-being of my community.

At home, I am a lover of books, music, food, video games, and the great outdoors.
